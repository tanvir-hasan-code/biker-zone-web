Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet is old and needs replacement, check its age (replace every 5-7 years), look for visible damage like cracks or dents, inspect the interior padding for wear or compression, and ensure it still fits snugly and securely. If any of these issues are present, it’s time for a new helmet.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding your bike after storage, check for flat tires, rusted chains, and ensure brakes and gears are functioning properly. If everything looks good and the bike is in working condition, it should be safe to ride.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet, follow traffic laws, use hand signals, and stay visible to others on the road.
